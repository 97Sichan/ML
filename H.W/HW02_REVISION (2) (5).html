df = pd.read_csv("iris.csv") # Read & Pick the data
df2= df[(df['Species'] == "virginica") | (df['Species'] == "versicolor")]
df2 = df2.loc[:,["Petal.Length","Species"]]

x = df2['Petal.Length'].to_numpy()
X = x.copy()
nrows = X.shape[0]
X.shape = (nrows, 1)

y = (df2['Species'] == 'virginica').to_numpy().astype(int)

model = linear_model.LogisticRegression(C=1000)
model.fit(X, y)
b = model.intercept_
w = model.coef_[0]
print(f'intercept={b}, slope={w}, training score={model.score(X, y)}')

1b. Do some work with logistic regression by hand.¶

Consider the logistic regression model, $P(y _i = 1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\mathbf{w x} + b)}}\,.$

Logistic regression is named after the log-odds of success, $\ln \frac{p}{1 - p}$, where $p = P(y_i = 1)$. Show that this log-odds equals $\mathbf{w x} + b$. (That is, start with $\ln \frac{p}{1 - p}$ and connect it in a series of equalities to $\mathbf{w x} + b$.)

$\begin{align*} % In this Latex context, "&" separates columns and "\\" ends a line. \ln \frac{p}{1 - p} & = \ln\frac{\frac{1}{1+e^{-(wx+b)}}}{\frac{e^{-(wx+b)}}{1+e^{-(wx+b)}}} \\ & -> (Multiply \ e^{(wx+b)} to \ each \ numerators \ and \ denominators)\\ & = \ln\frac{\frac{e^{(wx+b)}}{1+e^{(wx+b)}}}{\frac{1}{1+e^{(wx+b)}}} \\ & = \ln e^{wx+b}\\ & = \mathbf{w x} + b\\ \end{align*} $

2d. Do some decision tree calculations by hand.¶

Consider a decision tree node containing the following set of examples $S = \{(\mathbf{x}, y)\}$ where $\mathbf{x} = (x_1, x_2)$:

((4, 9), 1)

((2, 6), 0)

((5, 7), 0)

((3, 8), 1)

Find the entropy of $S$.

$H(x) = -[P(y=1)\log_2P(y=1) + P(y=0)\log_2P(y=0)] \\ \quad \quad = - [\frac{1}{2}\log_2(\frac{1}{2})+\frac{1}{2}\log_2(\frac{1}{2})] \\ \quad \quad = 1$

2e. Do some more decision tree calculations by hand.¶

Find a (feature, threshold) pair that yields the best split for this node.

The lower entropy indicates the best split.¶

If the feature is X2 and threshold is 7 (X2, 7),¶

$H_1(x) = -[P(y=1)\log_2P(y=1) + P(y=0)\log_2P(y=0)] \\ \quad \quad = - [1\log_2(1)+0\log_2(0)] \\ \quad \quad = 0 $

$H_2(x) = -[P(y=1)\log_2P(y=1) + P(y=0)\log_2P(y=0)] \\ \quad \quad = - [0\log_2(0)+1\log_2(1)] \\ \quad \quad = 0 $

  • (Where H_1(x) is entropy for X2 is the clasification under the condition of "greater than 7" and H_2(x) is entropy for X2 is the clasification under the condition of "less than or eqaul to 7.")

Therefore, (X2, 7) is the best split with the lowest entropy 0.¶
